ABSTRACT
Recently, Unsupervised algorithms has achieved remarkable performance in image dehazing. However, the
CycleGAN framework can lead to confusion in generator learning due to inconsistent data distributions, and
the DisentGAN framework lacks effective constraints on generated images, resulting in the loss of image content
details and color distortion. Moreover, Squeeze and Excitation channel attention employs only fully connected
layers to capture global information, lacking interaction with local information, resulting in inaccurate feature
weight allocation for image dehazing. To solve the above problems, in this paper, we propose an Unsupervised
Bidirectional Contrastive Reconstruction and Adaptive Fine-Grained Channel Attention Networks (UBRFCNet). Specifically, an Unsupervised Bidirectional Contrastive Reconstruction Framework (BCRF) is proposed,
aiming to establish bidirectional contrastive reconstruction constraints, not only to avoid the generator learning
confusion in CycleGAN but also to enhance the constraint capability for clear images and the reconstruction
ability of the unsupervised dehazing network. Furthermore, an Adaptive Fine-Grained Channel Attention
(FCA) is developed to utilize the correlation matrix to capture the correlation between global and local
information at various granularities promotes interaction between them, achieving more efficient feature
weight assignment. Experimental results on challenging benchmark datasets demonstrate the superiority of
our UBRFC-Net over state-of-the-art unsupervised image dehazing methods. This study successfully introduces
an enhanced unsupervised image dehazing approach, addressing limitations of existing methods and achieving
superior dehazing results. The source code is available at https://github.com/Lose-Code/UBRFC-Net.

1. Introduction
In recent years, with increasing environmental pollution and adverse weather conditions, due to the impact of haze, images captured by
optical sensors often suffer from quality issues such as color distortion,
blurring and low contrast. With the rapid development of deep learning
technology, advanced vision tasks (Luo, Zhang, Du, & Zhang, 2020; Sun
et al., 2021; Wan et al., 2020; Zhang, Song, Du, & Zhang, 2021) such as
object detection (Chen et al., 2020; Girshick, 2015; Hahner, Sakaridis,
Dai, & Van Gool, 2021; Khalid & Karan, 2023; Zhang & Zhang, 2022),
object tracking (Cheng et al., 2021; Kamkar, Moghaddam, Lashgari, &
Erlhagen, 2022; Yan et al., 2021; Yang, Zhang, Zhou, & Liu, 2021), and
image segmentation (Chen, Zhu, Papandreou, Schroff, & Adam, 2018;
Ding, Qiao, Yuille, & Shen, 2021; Shi, Orouskhani, & Orouskhani, 2021;

Vacher, Launay, & Coen-Cagli, 2022) are widely used in autonomous
driving, UAV inspection and security surveillance. The obtaining of
clear images as input for advanced vision tasks by image dehazing
methods (Bai, Pan, Xiang, & Tang, 2022; Bhandari, 2019; Li et al., 2021;
Zhang & Patel, 2018) in adverse weather conditions has gradually
become a hot topic of researchers.
Currently, supervised dehazing methods have achieved remarkable
results (Dong et al., 2020; Qu, Chen, Huang, & Xie, 2019; Sun, Li, et al.,
2023; Wu et al., 2021). However, there is a notable disparity between
the distribution of synthesized haze and real-world haze. Hence, researching unsupervised dehazing methods is of significant importance.
Researchers have proposed some unsupervised dehazing algorithms,
which can be mainly categorized into two types: the CycleGAN-based

& Nayar, 2000, 2002) is used to build an unsupervised dehazing
network from the clear images, transmission maps and atmospheric
light values output by the end-to-end network and the parameter
estimation network. Simultaneously, we leverage hazy images from
the dataset and outputs from both networks to establish bidirectional
contrastive reconstruction constraints, which not only effectively overcomes the challenges posed by different data distributions in the CycleGAN framework, mitigating generator learning confusion, but also
effectively supplementing constraints on image clarity and content.
This enhances the network’s reconstruction capabilities, strengthening
its dehazing performance. Additionally, we propose an adaptive finegrained channel attention mechanism. The correlation between global
and local information at various granularities is captured by using
cross-correlation operations to obtain correlation matrices. This systematically promotes interaction between global and local information,
accurately capturing their correlation at different granularities, and
better allocating reasonable weights for dehazing-related features.

2.1. Image dehazing methods
In the early stages of image dehazing research, scholars proposed
haze removal methods (Berman, Avidan, et al., 2016; Berman, Treibitz,
& Avidan, 2018) that relied on prior information. Among them, He,
Sun, and Tang (2010) proposed the dark channel prior, which assumes
that the locally lowest intensity in RGB channels should be close to
zero in haze-free natural images. Zhu, Mai, and Shao (2015) introduced
the color attenuation prior, they point that the difference between
the value and saturation of pixels should be positively correlated to
the depth of scene in a linear model. These methods often stemmed
from empirical observations of hazy images and employed manually designed heuristic rules and experiential knowledge for dehazing. While
these approaches have proven effective for image dehazing, their performance is limited due to the assumption of prior conditions that
might not hold true for all real-world images. Abdulkareem et al.
(2021) pointed out that the method based on the dark channel prior
in the restoration process results in images with missing details, exhibits unnatural coloring, and faces issues such as blur at the edges
of the image. Additionally, the dark channel prior is not effective
in handling scenes that include sky regions and white objects. Arif
et al. (2021) highlighted that machine learning-extracted features offer
a more general feature representation, are less time-consuming, costeffective, and exhibit universality for datasets compared to manually
extracted features. With the development of deep learning, its applications have gradually matured across various fields, driving continuous
technological innovation and advancing multiple domains such as artificial intelligence, computer vision, and natural language processing.

However, the CycleGAN-based methods suffer from a challenge:
the input to the generator includes data from both the dataset and
the generated data, leading to confusion in the learning process. More
recently, DisentGAN-based approaches supervised by GANs have attempted to address this issue by separating the clear components
from hazy images. Li et al. (2021), Yang et al. (2018), but these
algorithms only use a single constraint to impose clarity constraints
on end-to-end generated images, leading to insufficient constraints
and resulting in the loss of content details and color distortion in
the end-to-end generated images. In contrast, the proposed Unsupervised Bidirectional Contrastive Reconstruction Framework utilizes the
atmospheric scattering model to establish unsupervised bidirectional
contrastive reconstruction constraint between the parameter estimation
network and the end-to-end network. This effectively prevents the
generator from learning disparate input data distributions and enhances
constraint capability of the network.

Arif et al. (2022) proposed a deep learning-based model for multifoggy scene detection, achieving notable results and further confirming
the robust capabilities of deep learning. Therefore, in recent years,
researchers have begun to explore learning-based dehazing methods.
Currently, learning-based dehazing methods can be categorized into
two types: supervised dehazing methods and unsupervised dehazing
methods.
Supervised methods: Recently, in the field of image dehazing,
various supervised methods have emerged, which can be further categorized into parameter estimation methods and end-to-end dehazing
methods.
Parameter estimation-based dehazing methods use convolutional
neural networks to estimate parameters within the atmospheric scattering model (e.g., atmospheric light, transmission map) for the purpose of
dehazing. Cai, Xu, Jia, Qing, and Tao (2016) achieved image dehazing
by estimating the transmission map and establishing the mapping
relationship between hazy images and transmission map. Zhang and
Patel (2018) proposed a dense connection pyramid dehazing network
for estimating transmission map and atmospheric light for dehazing.
Bai et al. (2022) proposed a self-guided dehazing network based on a
progressive feature fusion technique by estimating transmission map.
While these parameter estimation-based dehazing methods have made
significant progress, they do not provide a more precise estimations
of atmospheric light and transmission map and may suffer from the
Cumulative error. Consequently, the recovered images obtained from
these methods frequently suffer from image degradation issues like
color distortion and missing details.
Currently, to address the challenges faced by parameter estimationbased dehazing methods, researchers have introduced end-to-end dehazing approaches. These approaches aim to establish directly the
mapping relationship between hazy and clear images, circumventing
the complexities of parameter estimation and thus overcoming issues
like color distortion and loss of details. In recent years, Qin et al.
(2020) proposed FFA-Net that fuses channel attention and pixel attention to improve the attention to hazy regions. Wu et al. (2021) proposed
an AECR-Net, a dehazing network on the basis of the contrastive
learning principles. This method utilizes hazy and clear images as
positive and negative samples to construct the contrastive loss, thereby
further enhancing the dehazing performance. Yu et al. (2021) introduced a two-branch dehazing approach using ensemble learning. This
method uses learnable fusion tails to map distinct features from the two
branches, achieving impressive dehazing performance on non-uniform
hazy datasets.
Unsupervised methods: Supervised training methods can improve
dehazing results, but they rely heavily on a substantial number of
paired images. However, obtaining realistic hazy images and their
corresponding clear counterparts is a challenging task. Currently, the
prevalent approach involves training networks using synthesized haze,
but there exists a disparity between the distribution of synthesized
haze and real-world haze. Hence, researching unsupervised dehazing
methods holds significant importance.
In recent years, researchers have leveraged the CycleGAN framework as a foundation to develop numerous unsupervised algorithms.
Engin et al. (2018) introduced an enhanced CycleGAN framework that
incorporates perceptual consistency loss to strengthen the network constraints, leading to a significant improvement in dehazing effectiveness.
Dudhane and Murala (2019) proposed a cycle consistent dehazing
network (CDNet) building upon the CycleGAN architecture. They employed the estimation of transmission maps for dehazing, achieving
remarkable dehazing results. Mehta, Sinha, Mandal, and Narang (2021)
proposed a SkyGAN for haze removal in aerial images. They enhanced
the dehazing performance of the network by incorporating a domainaware hazy-to-hyperspectral module and a conditional GAN based
multi-cue image-to-image translation module. The framework is illustrated in the following diagram. Liu, Hou, Duan, and Qiu (2020)

2.2. Channel attention
In recent years, the channel attention mechanism (Hu, Shen, &
Sun, 2018; Li, Wang, Hu, & Yang, 2019; Sun, Luo, et al., 2023; Wang
et al., 2020; Woo, Park, Lee, & Kweon, 2018) has garnered significant attention from researchers due to its ability to capture valuable
channel information, resulting in a notable enhancement of network
performance. Hu et al. (2018) introduced the squeeze-and-excitation
(SE) channel attention mechanism. This mechanism employs squeezing
and excitation operations to allocate weights to important feature
channels, consequently improving the performance of image classification networks. Woo et al. (2018) presented a convolutional block
attention module (CBAM), which surpasses the performance of SE by
combining both spatial attention and channel attention. This module
achieves superior image classification performance through the fusion
of these attention mechanisms. Li et al. (2019) introduced a selective
kernel (SK) channel attention mechanism that utilizes multiple sizes
of convolutional kernels. This approach has further improved classification performance by enhancing the channel attention mechanism
with the incorporation of various kernel sizes. Wang et al. (2020)
proposed an efficient channel attention (ECA) mechanism to address
the issue of negative effects caused by dimensionality reduction in
SE. This approach aims to improve the efficiency of channel attention
while mitigating the potential drawbacks of dimensionality reduction.
Currently, the SE channel attention has been widely applied in the
field of image dehazing and has achieved impressive dehazing effects.

Datasets: In this paper, we evaluate the synthetic hazy datasets
RESIDE (Lan et al., 2023), the real hazy datasets Dense-Haze (Sandler

Testing Set (SOTS) in Outdoor as our test set. OTS contains 8970
clear images and 313950 hazy images, and SOTS contains 500 outdoor
hazy images and 500 corresponding clear images. The Dense-Haze
dataset comprises 45 images with dense haze, among which 35 are
allocated for training, 5 for validation, and 5 for testing. The NHHaze2021 dataset consists of 25 images with non-uniform haze. Since
the validation and test images for this dataset have not been released
yet, we utilize 20 paired images for training and the remaining 5 pairs
for testing. The remote sensing dataset Haze1k comprises three subsets,
each representing scenes with different levels of haze concentration:
thin haze representing low concentration haze, medium haze representing moderate concentration haze, and thick haze representing high
concentration haze. Each subset’s training set contains 320 images, the
validation set includes 35 images, and the test set consists of 45 images.
Due to the scarcity of real-world hazy datasets and remote sensing data,
we augment the dataset by randomly cropping the images to a size of
256 × 256 pixels.

5. Discussion
Our UBRFC-Net outperforms the other eight unsupervised algorithms on both natural and remote sensing datasets, demonstrating excellent performance. This advantage can be attributed to the establishment of bidirectional contrastive reconstruction constraints, addressing
constraints on image clarity and content to enhance the network’s
reconstruction capabilities. Additionally, by promoting interaction between global and local channel information, we achieve better weight
allocation for dehazing-related features. However, our method has
limitations, as the parameter estimation network still encounters inaccuracies in haze removal. Further optimization is needed to enhance
the constraints and form a better end-to-end dehazing network. Additionally, the lightweight design of model parameters is an aspect that
requires further consideration for improvement.
