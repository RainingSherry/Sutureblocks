Abstract

Capitalizing on the talent of self-attention in capturing
non-local features, Transformer architectures have exhibited remarkable performance in single hyperspectral image restoration. For hyperspectral images, each pixel
is located in the hyperspectral image cubes with a large
spectral dimension and two spatial dimensions. Although
uni-dimensional self-attention, like channel self-attention
or spatial self-attention, builds long-range dependencies
in spectral or spatial dimensions, they lack more comprehensive interactions across dimensions. To tackle the
above drawback, we propose a VolFormer, a volumetric
self-attention embedded Transformer network for single hyperspectral image restoration. Speciﬁcally, we propose volumetric self-attention (VolSA), which extends the interaction from 2D ﬂat to 3D cube. VolSA can simultaneously
model token interaction in the 3D cube, mining the potential correlations between the hyperspectral image cube.
An attention decomposition form is proposed to reduce
the computational burden of modeling volumetric information. In practical terms, VolSA adapts double similarity matrixes in spatial and channel dimensions to implicitly model 3D context information while transforming the
complexity from cubic to quadratic. Additionally, we introduce the explicit spectral location prior to enhance the
proposed self-attention. This property allows the target token to perceive global spectral information while simultaneously assigning different levels of attention to tokens at
varying wavelength bands. Extensive experiments demonstrate that VolFormer achieves record-high performance on
hyperspectral image super-resolution, denoise and classiﬁcation benchmarks. The source code is available at
https://github.com/yudadabing/VolFormer.

1. Introduction
Hyperspectral image (HSI) is referred to as an image cube,
which has a large spectral dimension besides spatial dimension. Large spectral dimension provide ample and detailed
spectral information to identify and distinguish objects.
Therefore, hyperspectral images have been widely used in
various tasks. [33, 37]. Due to the physical limitations of
imaging sensors and long imaging time, the captured hyperspectral images maintain low resolution and introduce
noise. Thus, hyperspectral image restoration is mainly categorized into hyperspectral image super-resolution and hyperspectral image denoise, which aims to restore a highquality image from the degraded observation.
Deep learning methods based on data-driven have explicit performance over conventional prior-based methods
in most computer vision tasks. Thus, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) have been used in HSI restoration
tasks and demonstrate the powerful spatial-spectral feature extraction ability. However, the local feature learning characteristic of prevailing CNN methods shows limitations in modeling non-local correlation. Extracting nonlocal features is equally signiﬁcant in high-dimensional data
of hyperspectral images. Vision Transformer utilizes selfattention to capture long-range dependencies and provide
powerful local and non-local feature representations. Recent work [56, 62] makes an effort to tailor the vision Transformer for hyperspectral image restoration tasks. These
Transformers exploit the self-attention mechanisms (i.e.,
channel self-attention and spatial self-attention) to extract
the non-local features in spatial and spectral dimensions and
signiﬁcantly improve visual quality and quantitative metrics. However, in channel self-attention (CSA) or spatial
self-attention (SSA), the three-dimensional (3D) context information is mapped to a two-dimensional (2D) similarity matrix, where space or channel compression. Different
from traditional visible images with R-G-B channels, hy-

acquire images at a nominal spectral resolution, HSIs have a
continuous, narrowband spectrum. The continuous spectral
channels represent that the spectral correlations are closely
related to wavelength. That is, the closer the spectral channels are to each other, the more similar their spectral wavelengths and the reﬂectance information they capture will
be. However, the traditional self-attention’s inherent structure lacks explicit location priors. In our volumetric selfattention, the explicit spectrum location prior is added to 2D
similarity matrixes in the spectral domain, i. e., for a target

degree of correlation. Self-attention’s quadratic complexity leads to signiﬁcant computational costs when extending
the pixel interaction to the cube. To relieve the computational costs, in the proposed volumetric self-attention, we
follow the volume of cube calculation paradigm. The cube

Our contributions can be summarized as follows:
• We propose the VolFormer for hyperspectral image
restoration. Extensive experimental results on hyperspectral super-resolution and denoise show that our VolFormer
obtains notable performance in both objective metrics and
subjective visual quality.
• We have tailored the volumetric self-attention for the
hyperspectral images, which extends the long-range dependency into three-dimensional space and maintains the
quadratic complexity.
• Considering the characteristics of the continuous spectrum, we introduce an explicit spectrum location prior to
the volumetric self-attention, which employs a bidirectional decay matrix to describe the correlation of spectral
channels.

CNNs have been introduced into restoration tasks for single hyperspectral images and have numerous variations
[7, 18, 19, 23, 29, 38, 50, 60]. Since hyperspectral image
contains abundant spectral information, 3D CNNs [11, 45]
and mixed 2D/3D convolutional networks [26, 27] are designed to reconstruct the HSI, which extracts the spectral

Transformer has been an effective alternative to CNNs in
vision applications, e.g., image recognition [8], segmentation [46], and object detection [5]. The vision Transformer
model (ViT) proposed by Dosovitskiy et al. [8] applies a
pure Transformer directly to sequences of image patches to
classify the full image and achieves state-of-the-art performance. Naturally, Transformer has been developed to address low-level vision problems, such as image restoration.
Liang et al. [30] proposed the SwinIR model for image
restoration based on the Swin Transformer to apply selfattention within local image regions. Restormer [59] captures long-range channel interactions and achieves pleasing
results on several image restoration tasks. Transformer’s
superior ability to capture long-term information and relationships inspires its application in the ﬁeld of hyperspectral
image restoration [24, 25, 56, 62] and reconstruction[4, 44].
The previous works aim to calculate self-attention along the
spectral and spatial dimensions to capture long-range spectrum dependencies and explore intrinsic similarity characteristics. However, existing Transformer-based methods
lack the joint analysis between spectral and spatial dimensions.

3.2. Volumetric Self-Attention
The RGB image is similar to a sandwich with triplethin layers corresponding to discrete R-G-B spectral channels. While the hyperspectral image can be seen as
a pumpernickel with continuous spectra. Existing selfattention paradigms, including spatial self-attention and
channel self-attention, only model non-local correlation
for two-dimensional slices that are difﬁcult to mine comprehensive correlations hidden in the hyperspectral image
cube. Thus, we propose volumetric self-attention (VolSA),
a novel three-dimensional self-attention paradigm. VolSA
employs the two 2D similarity matrixes corresponding to
spectral-wise projection and spatial-wise projection to implicitly build the long-range dependencies in a cube with the
relay communication way.

4.1. Super-Resolution Experimental Evaluation
Experimental Settings. In this section, we evaluate our
VolFormer both qualitatively and quantitatively on natural HSI dataset, the CAVE dataset [54] and two remote
sensing HSI datasets, the Pavia dataset and the Chikusei dataset [55]. DIV2K [1] is adopted for the auxiliary
RGBI SR task. The results of the proposed method on the
datasets are compared with seven state-of-the-art methods,
i.e., GDRNN[29], MCNet[26], ERCSR[27], SSPSR[19],
HSISR[23], DSTrans[56], and ESSA [62]. In the HSI SR
task, we focus on upscaling factors ×4 and ×8 for comparison in the experiment. All of the experiments presented
in this paper are performed on NVIDIA GeForce RTX
3090 GPUs. More information about the dataset and implement details are presented in Appendix 8.1 and 8.2.
Metrics. The quantitative evaluation of SR performance
is based on six standard metrics, i.e., spectral angle mapper (SAM) [58], cross correlation (CC) [32], erreur relative
globale adimensionnelle de synthese (ERGAS) [43], root
mean squared error (RMSE), mean peak signal-to-noise ratio (MPSNR), and mean structure similarity (MSSIM) [47].

Discussion of the Self-Attention Mechanisms. To verify the effectiveness of the proposed VolSA, we have conducted an ablation study of several self-attention mechanisms, and the results are demonstrated in Table 5. We compare our VolSA with spatial self-attention(SSA) and channel self-attention (CSA). We also compare our VolSA with
the linear combination form (SSA+CSA) of spatial selfattention and channel self-attention. Additionally, we have
analyzed the effect of the proposed spectrum location prior.
VolSA w/o SLP denotes the VolSA without spectrum location prior. Table 5 demonstrates that our VolSA provides
the most competitive gain over the SSA, CSA, and the combined form. Furthermore, the spectrum location prior results in the expected improvement, and the cost of computational complexity can be negligible. As depicted in Fig.
5, the LAM [13] results and DI [13] results indicate that
our VolSA has the most further interaction distance and utilizes the most pixels located over the HSI cube to restore
correct and clear textures. More ablation studies and dis-

cussion about speciﬁc architecture and computational cost
can be found in Appendix 11 and 12.

5. Conclusion
This paper presents an efﬁcient Transformer, called
VolFormer, for single hyperspectral image restoration,
including super-resolution and denoising.
VolFormer
is capable of modeling more comprehensive interaction in the hyperspectral image cube without increasing
largely computing costs. Speciﬁcally, we propose a
particular volumetric self-attention to model 3D context
information by relaying similarity matrixes in spatial
and channel dimensions. Beneﬁting from the particular
design, VolFormer transforms the complexity from cubic
to quadratic. Besides, we utilize the spectrum characteristics in HSI and introduce the location prior to improve
the representation ability of the self-attention mechanism. Extensive experiments for HSI super-resolution
and denoising demonstrate the SOTA performance
of VolFormer in terms of visual and quantitative results.
