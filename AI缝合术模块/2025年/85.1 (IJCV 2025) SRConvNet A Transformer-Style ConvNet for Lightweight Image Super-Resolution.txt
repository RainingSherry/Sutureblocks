Abstract
Recently, vision transformers have demonstrated their superiority against convolutional neural networks (ConvNet) in various
tasks including single-image super-resolution (SISR). The success of transformers can be attributed to the indispensable multihead self-attention (MHSA) mechanism, which enables to effectively model global connectivity with fewer parameters.
However, the quadratic complexity of MHSA usually encounters vast computation costs and memory resource occupation,
limiting their efficient deployment on mobile devices compared to widely used lightweight ConvNets. In this work, we
thoroughly explore the key differences between ConvNet- and transformer-based SR models, thus presenting SRConvNet
that absorbs both the merits for lightweight SISR. Our SRConvNet is accomplished by two primary designs: (1) the Fourier
modulated attention (FMA), an MHSA-like but more computationally and parametrically efficient operator that performs
regional frequency-spatial modulation and aggregation to ensure long-term and short-term dependencies modeling; (2) the
dynamic mixing layer (DML) utilizing mixed-scale depthwise dynamic convolution with channel splitting and shuffling to
explore multi-scale contextualized information for model locality and adaptability enhancement. Combining FMA and DFN,
we can build a pure transformer-style ConvNet to compete with the best lightweight SISR models in the trade-off between
efficiency and accuracy. Extensive experiments demonstrate that SRConvNet can achieve more efficient SR reconstruction
than recent state-of-the-art lightweight SISR methods on both computation and parameters while preserving comparable
performance. Code is available at https://github.com/lifengcs/SRConvNet.
Keywords Lightweight image super-resolution · Fourier modulated attention · Dynamic mixing layer

1 Introduction
Single image super-resolution (SISR) aims at recovering a
high-resolution (HR) image from its degraded low-resolution
(LR) counterpart, which is a fundamental and longstanding
problem in computer vision and has attracted much attention in various applications (Wang et al., 2021b; Zhang et
al., 2019b; Amirpour et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2022; Guo et al.,

2018; Zhou et al., 2022a, 2023). The monumental progress
in SISR mainly concentrates on convolutional neural networks (ConvNets)-based methods (Dong et al., 2016b; Kim
et al., 2016b; Lai et al., 2017; Lim et al., 2017; Zhang et
al., 2018b; Wang et al., 2018; Zhang et al., 2018a, 2020;
Wang et al., 2021d) in the past years. From the pioneering
3-layer SISR network SRCNN (Dong et al., 2016b) to a representative very deep models RCAN (Zhang et al., 2018a)

architectures to pursue large receptive field and hierarchical feature learning for SR performance improvements. The
effectiveness of these models can attribute to their huge learnable network parameters and expensive computational cost.
As a result, it is challenging to deploy current heavy SR
models on resource-constrained mobile and edge devices. To
be efficient, mainstream works focus on reducing the model
complexity by recursive learning (Tai et al., 2017b, a; Li et
al., 2019), lightweight module design (Ahn et al., 2018; Li
et al., 2020b; Hui et al., 2019; Luo et al., 2023), or structural
re-parameterization (Zhang et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022b).
On one hand, these methods ensure their efficiency at the
cost of sacrificing SR performance. On the other hand, due
to their limited convolution layers and small spatial convolution, the inadequate receptive field exploration also restricts
the learning capacity for better reconstruction.
More recently, vision transformers (ViT) (Dosovitskiy et
al., 2021; Liu et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2021c; Guo et al.,
2022c; Zhou et al., 2022b) have demonstrated impressive
effectiveness in various tasks including SISR (Chen et al.,
2021; Liang et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2022b; Li et al., 2022).
Benefiting from the strong ability of multi-head self-attention
(MHSA) mechanism for global dependencies modeling,
ViTs enable to develop deep architectures with fewer parameters but better results compared to ConvNets. Nevertheless,
the computation cost of MHSA grows quadratically as input
spatial size increases, especially when super-resolving largeresolution images, will inevitably withstand large memory
resource occupation. Such quadratic computation indicates
that it is impractical to generalize ViTs to various applications for mobile-friendly inference.
Despite the relatively inferior performance of ConvNets
compared to ViTs, conventional convolutions perceive localized receptive fields and share all convolutional weights over
the whole feature map, endowing ConvNets with favorable
inductive biases for image processing (Dai et al., 2021; Zhang
et al., 2022), in particular on pixel-level tasks. Though some
recent attempts have introduced convolution into ViTs (Wu
et al., 2021; Li et al., 2021; Lu1 et al., 2022; Zhang et al.,
2022) to yield both local and global connectivity and further
implement efficient yet effective hybrid architectures, to our
best knowledge, ConvNets still dominate in the lightweight
SISR area. These observations inspired us whether we can
combine the merits of both ConvNets and ViTs to overcome
the resource restriction of mobile devices while preserving
high-quality SR reconstruction.
To this end, we revisit the above two different design
paradigms and present a pure transformer-style ConvNet,
termed as SRConvNet, for lightweight SISR. Specifically,
we first present Fourier modulated attention (FMA) to mimic
the MHSA in ViTs for long-term and short-term dependen-

cies modeling. The FMA is motivated by three perspectives:
(1) The element-wise multiplication between value features
and large-kernel convolutional features can effectively evoke
global spatial attention but involves cheaper linear computation (Guo et al., 2022b, a; Hou et al., 2022). (2) Fourier
spectrum in the frequency domain allows capturing the global
receptive field over an entire image, even in early layers (Suvorov et al., 2022; Chi et al., 2020). (3) The window
attention scheme is capable of local- and global-aware feature learning (Liu et al., 2021). Based on these observations,
FMA patchifies the features into a grid of non-overlapped
local regions and replaces the large-kernel convolution with
Fourier transform and inverse transform to perform regional
frequency-spatial modulation. In this way, FMA can learn
context from global to local in spatial and channel dimensions.
The other core component in SRConvNet is the proposed dynamic mixing layer (DML) which incorporates
the mixed-scale dynamic convolutional mechanism between
channel splitting and shuffling operations to better aggregate local feature representations. Our dynamic convolutions
in multi-scale form can not only learn multi-scale contexts
but also generate dynamic weights to fortify the adaptability
of conventional convolutions. Besides, being equipped with
channel splitting and shuffling operations, DML enables efficient local contextual information mixing and interactions.
By reformulating the transformer block with the combination
of FMA and DML, SRConvNet achieves the optimal balance
between accuracy and model complexity (see Fig. 1).
The main contributions are summarized as follows:
• We propose SRConvNet which absorbs the advantages
of ConvNets and ViTs for lightweight SISR. Experimental results demonstrate remarkable efficiency and
competitive reconstruction performance over existing
state-of-the-art methods.
• We propose a Fourier modulated attention (FMA) that
performs regional frequency-spatial modulation to model
long- and short-term dependencies under a cheaper computational cost than typical MHSA.
• We propose a dynamic mixing layer (DML) that can
capture multi-scale local contextual information while
enhancing network adaptability efficiently.

adversarial training to pursue photo-realistic HR image generation. Among these methods, ESRGAN (Wang et al., 2018)
combines very deep architecture and complex constraints
to obtain a better trade-off between perceptual quality and
accuracy. Although these methods have achieved promising
results, the high computational resource requirement limits
their deployment on mobile devices.

2.2 Efficient SISR Methods
Efficient or lightweight model designs have become essential research areas in the development of deep learning-based
SISR methods. Tai et al. (2017b, a); Kim et al. (2016a) combines residual and recursive learning to implement deep
models without introducing extra parameters. CARN (Ahn
et al., 2018) utilizes group convolution to constitute an efficient cascaded residual network for both parameter and
computation saving. Chu et al. (2020) exploit elastic neural
architecture search to draw a lightweight SR network that is
competitive with CARN. IMDN (Hui et al., 2019) formulates
an information multi-distillation block with contrast-aware
attention to achieve fast and accurate SISR with modest parameters. In Li et al. (2020a), a linearly-assembled
pixel-adaptive regression network is presented to learn linear combination coefficients of predefined filter bases for
every input pixel. Wang et al. (2021a) explore both channel
and spatial sparsity to remove the redundant computation
in flat regions, thus improving the inference efficiency of
SR networks. Motivated by the re-parameterization strategy in high-level tasks (Ding et al., 2019, 2021), several
methods (Zhang et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022b) study the
structure re-parameterization technique to realize effective
SR reconstruction on commodity mobile devices. Park et al.
(2023) propose the dynamic residual attention networks to
explore the interrelation between residual paths and input
image statistics, which shows an efficient balance model
complexity and performance. Wang et al. (2023) introduce
global aligned structured sparsity learning to prune extensive
residual blocks, improving the efficiency of SISR models.

2.3 Transformer in SISR

3.1 Network Architecture

Transformer is first introduced in natural language processing (NLP) (Vaswani et al., 2017; Devlin et al., 2019; Brown
et al., 2020) to draw global dependencies in sequences based
solely on self-attention. The breakthroughs of transformer in
NLP has rapidly aroused great interest in the computer vision
community, raising a research wave on transformer-based
vision tasks (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021; Carion et al., 2020).
Chen et al. (2021) develop a pre-trained transformer for
image processing tasks including SISR. Liang et al. (2021)
present SwinIR which adapts the swin transformer (Liu et
al., 2021) to learn deep features for the SR problem, surpassing current state-of-the-art ConvNet-based methods. Li
et al. (2022) investigate window-based image processing
pre-training transformers and propose an efficient encoderdecoder transformer for data exploitation and computation.
ELAN (Zhang et al., 2022) design a group-wise multiscale self-attention (GMSA) to exploit the long-range image
dependency by calculating the SA of different window sizes.
Attention sharing strategy is further applied to accelerate
the computation for successive GMSA modules, resulting
in higher efficiency than SwinIR. ESRT (Lu1 et al., 2022)
adopts a hybrid SISR model consisting of a lightweight ConvNet Backbone and an efficient multi-head attention-based
lightweight transformer backbone to learn the relationships
between local image regions. In Choi et al. (2023), NGram context is introduced from the text to SISR, which
is able to expand the receptive field of SR networks for
degraded pixel recovery by sliding window self-attention.
While recent attempts have successfully alleviated the computational burden of transformers to some extent, they are
still not preferable to SISR architectures than ConvNets to
support great resource-constrained applications.
Different from above mentioned related works, this
paper investigates the possibility of transformers in practical applications and combines it with ConvNets to design a
lightweight transformer-style SRConvNet, competing with
existing lightweight SISR models in the balance between
efficiency and effectiveness.

3.2 Fourier Modulated Attention

(ii) Spectrum to spatial: 2D-discrete inverse RealFFT

Recent works (Guo et al., 2022a; Hou et al., 2022) have
demonstrated that the network with large kernel attention
(LKA) using element-wise multiplication enables a large
receptive field to model long-range dependencies as ViTs.
Compared to the quadratic computation of MHSA, such
LKA can yield comparable recognition accuracy with linear complexity. Motivated by this design principle, as shown
in Fig. 2b, Fourier modulated attention (FMA) performs
regional frequency-spatial modulation to learn context from
local to global in spatial and channel dimensions, achieving
long- and short-term dependencies modeling.
To be specific, in FMA, we first pair Fourier transform
and 1 × 1 convolution that projects spatial features into the
spectral domain to learn frequency information. An inverse
Fourier transform is further leveraged to re-project frequency
features into the spatial domain. According to spectrum convolution theorem (Chi et al., 2019), our Fourier-Conv-Inverse
Fourier can effectively manipulate the entire image-level
spectrum, so that is capable of capturing global receptive
field. This process can be formulated as follows:

3.3 Dynamic Mixing Layer
Vanilla transformers (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021; Liu et al.,
2021; Liang et al., 2021) exploit linear layers as the feedforward network (FFN), which limits the capability of
networks to learn local context. Some works (Zhang et al.,
2022; Zamir et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022a) introduce singlescale depthwise convolution into FFN to improve the network
locality. However, these two both fail to learn multi-scale representations. Though (Gu et al., 2022) present mixed-scale
convolutional FFN (MixCFN) to increase the multi-scale
local information extraction, due to the static weight of convolution, it lacks channel adaptability in channel dimension.

3.4 Implementation Details
In the proposed SRConvNet, there are a total of 8 ACBs and
each ACB contains 1 FMA and 1 DML. The head number
in all FMAs is equally set to 8. GELU is adopted as the
activation function after convolution. The channel number
of all intermediate layers is 64. We train our model using L 1
loss (Eq. (4)) with Adam (Kingma & Ba, 2015) optimizer

International Journal of Computer Vision (2025) 133:173–189

(β1 = 0.9, β2 = 0.99). During training, we randomly crop
each LR RGB image into a 64 × 64 patch with a mini-batch
size of 128 for all scale factors. We first train a ×2 model for
1000 epochs with the initial learning rate 2 × 10−4 which
is then reduced by half at [800, 900, and 950] epochs. Then
the well-trained ×2 model is used to initialize ×3 and ×4
models and train them for 500 epochs. The initial rate is also
set to 2×10−4 but halved at [250, 400, 450, 475] epochs. Our
SRConvNet is implemented with the Pytorch framework on
Nvidia GeForce RTX A6000 GPUs for all experiments.

4 Experiments
In this section, we conduct quantitative and qualitative
comparisons with existing state-of-the-art lightweight SISR
methods to validate the efficiency and effectiveness of the
proposed SRConvNet. Then, we explore how SRConvNet
works on other image restoration tasks, including Gaussian
color image denoising and motion deblurring. Finally, we
carry out comprehensive ablation studies to investigate the
key design methodologies of each component in SRConvNet.

4.1 Dataset Setup
Following existing methods (Liang et al., 2021; Li et al.,
2020a), we adopt the widely used DF2K dataset to train our
models. DF2K dataset is a merged dataset that contains 2640
high-quality images from the Flickr2K (Lim et al., 2017)
dataset and 800 images from the DIV2K dataset (Timofte
et al., 2017). LR images for scale factors 2, 3, and 4 are
generated by bicubic downscaling their corresponding HR
images. Data augmentation including random rotation and
horizontal flipping is adopted to improve the model generalization. We evaluate SRConvNet on five benchmark datasets:
Set5 (Bevilacqua et al., 2012), Set14 (Zeyde et al., 2012),
BSD100 (Arbeláez et al., 2011), Urban100 (Huang et al.,
2015), and Manga109 (Matsui et al., 2017). All the results are
measured by the peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR) and structural similarity (SSIM) on the Y-channel from the YCbCr
color space. Besides, the parameters (Params.) and computational costs (FLOPs) of networks are reported to indicate the
model complexity. Here, we calculate the FLOPs by superresolving an LR image to its HR version of the size 1280×720
on all scale factors.

Effect of FMA. FMA is an MHSA-like attention mechanism that contains spatial-Fourier feature interaction by
“RealFFT-Conv-IRealFFT” operation, patch-based regional
modulation, and multi-head implementation. To explore the
contribution of FMA to SR reconstruction, we first build
a baseline model (Model A) by removing all the above
components. Then, we gradually add them to the baseline model, resulting in another 3 models. As shown in
Table 5, it is natural that Model A without any component but only 1 × 1 convolutions performs worst. Due to
the global characteristic of Fourier spectrum convolution,
incorporating spatial-Fourier feature interaction (Model B)
enables learning global context at early layers, thus obtaining higher PSNR/SSIM scores. It can also be seen that Model
C achieves better SR results than Model B. This is because
Model C combines spatial-Fourier interaction with regional
modulation, so that is able to capture both long-term and
short-term dependencies, improving the network capability
for SR reconstruction. In our full model (Model D), we
introduce the multi-head scheme in the regional modulation process to mimic the MHSA mechanism in ViTs, which
achieves the best PSNR/SSIM performance.

5 Conclusion
In this paper, we combine the merits of ViTs and ConvNets
to design the SRConvNet, which is a pure transformer-style
ConvNet for lightweight SISR. Specifically, we propose the
Fourier modulated attention (FMA) mechanism that conducts spatial-Fourier feature interaction and patch-based
regional modulation to learn context from global to local
in spatial and channel dimensions, thus effectively modeling
the long-term and short-term dependencies. Furthermore, we
also present the dynamic mixing layer (DML) to capture
multi-scale local contextual information while enhancing
network adaptability. Extensive experiments demonstrate
that SRConvNet can compete with the recent state-of-the-art
lightweight SISR methods in the trade-off between efficiency
and accuracy.
