ABSTRACT
Cracks are an earlier indication of severe structural damage, and are also an important indicator in the
evaluation of structural health and monitoring processes. However, the complex background interference
makes the segmentation of small cracks are an extremely challenging task. Therefore, a Dual Encoder
Crack Segmentation Network (DECS-Net) based on convolutional neural network (CNN) and transformer is
constructed to achieve automated crack detection. Firstly, a High–Low frequency Attention (HLA) mechanism is
proposed that uses the Haar wavelet to obtain the approximate and detailed components, and further processes
to obtain low-frequency and high-frequency features. In addition, a Locally Enhanced Feedforward Network
(LEFN) is designed to help the network improve its local information perception ability. Secondly, a Features
Fusion Module (FFM) is proposed to fuse the local features extracted by the CNN encoder and the global
contextual features extracted by the transformer encoder, implemented cross-domain fusion and correlation
enhancement. Finally, experiments are conducted on two public datasets DeepCrack and Crack3238, and
the recall showed a remarkable improvement, with 92.70% on DeepCrack and 79.02% on Crack3238. Its
comprehensive performance is ahead of the ten state-of-the-art models.

1. Introduction
Cracks are an earlier indication of severe damage to roads, bridges,
walls, and many other concrete structures. Outside the fields of civil engineering and transportation, micro-cracks detection in electronic components is also very important for the manufacturing industry (Cheng,
Xu et al., 2022). If cracks are not inspected and repaired in time,
it will seriously affect the service life of relevant infrastructure (He
et al., 2022). Most countries still use manual methods to detect cracks,
which is time-consuming and labor-intensive. In addition, the cost is
extremely high and has strong subjective factors. Therefore, achieving
automated detection is very necessary (Su et al., 2024). However,
cracks are often small strips and have complex backgrounds. So, it
is difficult to design a robust method. Deep learning based detection
methods (Wang et al., 2024) have become the mainstream nowadays, it

has stronger anti-interference ability. Researchers prefer to use semantic segmentation (Golding et al., 2022) in detect cracks. Firstly, classification algorithms only determine whether there are cracks (Liu et al.,
2022b), which cannot convey information about cracks to engineers.
Furthermore, object detection algorithm can only determine approximate crack position (Zhang, Wang et al., 2023), which will include
the background area. Segmentation algorithm can obtain pixel level
results, which is helpful for subsequent information processing (Liu
et al., 2022a), as shown in Fig. 1.
Convolutional neural network (CNN) is widely used in various detection tasks (Song et al., 2024; Xu et al., 2024) because of its excellent
ability to extract local informations. For example, Yan et al. (2022)

2. Related work
2.1. Convolutional neural network
The research of CNN has developed for many years, and many excellent algorithms (Zhang, Huang et al., 2023; Zhang, Zheng et al., 2022;
Zhang, Zou et al., 2022) have been born. Depthwise separable convolution (DSC) (Sifre & Mallat, 2014) is one of the significant developments,
which divides standard convolution into depthwise convolution (DW)
and pointwise convolution (PW) for calculation separately. It can significantly reduce computational complexity and parameters, and improve
model inference speed. Famous Xception (Chollet, 2017) and MobileNet (Howard et al., 2017) are built based on the classic DSC and
have achieved excellent performance. However, during the dimension
reduction process, researchers find that using inverted depthwise separable convolutions (IDSC) (i.e. PW followed by DW) can generate fewer
parameters.
The emergence of sub-pixel convolution (i.e. PixelShuffle) (Shi
et al., 2016) enables more information to be carried during upsampling.
In the past, when using transposed convolution (Dumoulin & Visin,
2016), there is a large amount of padding, which may be unfavorable
to the results. However, sub-pixel convolution combines single pixel on
multiple channels, translating them into sub-pixels on new features.
In the CNN-based crack segmentation network, SDDNet (Choi &
Cha, 2020) used DSC as the main unit, which has fewer parameters
and faster inference speed. DeepCrack (Zou et al., 2019) was a classic
model in crack segmentation, which outputs multi-scale feature maps
and fuses them. Xiang et al. (2022) designed a crack segmentation

As shown in Fig. 2. DECS-Net has two different encoders that extract
image features separately. First, the initialization layer, max pooling
layer, and four convolutional layers in ResNet-50 are separated to
build CNN encoder branch. Then, a novel transformer encoder branch
is designed to extract contextual information from crack images. The
DECS-Net is divided into four layers, and the features extracted from
the corresponding layers of the two encoders will be jointly input into
FFM to obtain the fused features. Fang et al. (2022) conducted sufficient
experiments and determined that the number of transformer block in
the encoder was 12. Therefore, we set that each layer will execute
transformer block 3 times. The transformer block of each layer are
composed of HLA and LEFN together, as shown in Fig. 3.
In order to adapt ResNet-50, in the transformer encoder, the DECSNet first divides the images into a set of 4 × 4 patches. Subsequently,
after each layer, these patches are merged to reduce the image size
to half the previous size. From top to bottom, feature maps with
downsampling of 4, 8, 16, and 32 times will be obtained.
The DECS-Net adopts a decoder, and the fused features of the
last layer are used as original input for the decoder. It is divided
into three layers in total, and each layer receives fused features of
corresponding scales. Firstly, sub-pixel convolution is performed on the
input features, which is an upsampling method that can better preserve
information. Then, the upsampled features are concatenated with the
fused features in the channel direction. After that, the IDSC is used to
reduce the dimension, and aggregate the features through a transformer
block. Finally, the feature maps is magnified 4 times to its original
size through sub-pixel convolution, and the final segmentation mask
is obtained through a 1 × 1 convolution.

3.3. Locally enhanced feedforward network (LEFN)
In traditional transformer, feedforward network is often composed
of several linear transformations and several nonlinear activation function. The vectors are projected to higher dimensions for extracting
richer semantic information. The LEFN is proposed to replace the
traditional feedforward network, as shown in Fig. 5.
First, spatial restoration is performed on the input vector and transform it into 2-dimensional space. Then, the DSC is used for dimensional
transformation to project features to a higher dimension. In high dimensional space, the DSC is adopted to extract image features, which
can generate local interactions between elements and enhance the
correlation between elements. Some features of images in low dimensional space cannot be well extracted. In high dimensional space,
richer semantic features can be well extracted. After each operation,
nonlinear factors are introduced through the GELU activation function
to improve the expression ability of model. Finally, features re-project
to the original space through IDSC, GELU, and batch normalization
(BN), and flatten it into a vector.
Replacing the fully connected layer with the DSC and the IDSC
not only enhances the interaction between vector elements, but also
reduces the parameters. Moreover, Mask2Former (Cheng, Misra et al.,
2022) proposed that the dropout layer is not necessary and can reduce
the performance of model in most cases. Therefore, the LEFN also
abandon this module.

4.1.1. DeepCrack
DeepCrack is a public benchmark dataset with cracks in multiple
scales and scenes to evaluate the crack detection systems. The dataset
has a total of 537 annotated images. Among them, 300 images are used
for training, and the remaining 237 images are used for testing. The
images in this dataset are all sampled from pavement cracks in various
background environments.
4.1.2. Crack3238
The images in Crack3238 are selected from four different opensource concrete-crack datasets. In order to increase the complexity
of crack detection scenarios, four datasets are combined for training
and testing. The combined dataset is randomly divided into training,
validation, and test sets in an 8:1:1 ratio, with each set generating 2590,
324, and 324 images, respectively. The mean damage ratio (MDR) of
crack images in four datasets are within 6%, indicating a relatively
small proportion of damage.

5. Conclusion
This paper proposed DECS-Net based on CNN and transformer,
adopting a dual encoder structure. First, a HLA mechanism is proposed. By using Haar wavelet decomposition, the detailed and approximate components of the image are obtained, thereby extracting
high-frequency and low-frequency features. High-frequency features
can highlight the edge information of cracks, while low-frequency features focus on global semantics. In addition, a novel LEFN is designed
to enhance the interaction between various patches, and improve the
local perception ability of the network. Most importantly, the FFM is
proposed to fuse the features extracted by CNN encoder and transformer encoder. For features between different domains, alignment is
first performed, followed by deep interaction between the two. It can
effectively integrate the advantages of CNN and transformer, making
the network more focused on the target and eliminating redundant
features through CA. Finally, experiments is conducted on two public
datasets DeepCrack and Crack3238, and compared proposed network
with ten other established models. From both performance metrics and
visual perspective, the proposed network has achieved better performance. Sufficient ablation experiments also are conducted, comparing
the different combination of components in FFM, number of different
encoder, different transformer block execution times, and different CNN
backbones to verify the effectiveness of the proposed modules.
However, the current our work is also limited. The proposed model
not utilize crack depth information, and the addition of depth information can undoubtedly enhance segmentation performance. Currently, a
large number of researches also lack this work. In the future, we will
consider introducing the currently popular large models and developing
a robust algorithm for multi-tasking scenarios. At the same time, we
will integrate multi-modal data to achieve synchronous measurement
of crack depth.
