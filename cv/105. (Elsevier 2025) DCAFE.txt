ABSTRACT
Precise medicinal flower classification is the prerequisite for their significant implications in healthcare,
pharmaceuticals, and food and cosmetic industries. Also, understanding the medicinal flower species is
an indispensable requirement for preserving and safeguarding biological diversity. However, the in-field
medicinal flower classification is a challenging task due to relatively large intra-class and small inter-class
variances and visual similarities with other tree categories. Nowadays, numerous deep learning methods,
especially Convolution Neural Networks have been applied to several image-based target classification tasks.
However, the existing methods fail to capture the complex feature maps such as petal patterns and flower
structural variations. The article proposes a novel ‘‘Flora-NET’’ method, first introducing coordinate attention
in conjunction with the involution neural network in the flower industry for classification tasks. The proposed
Flora-NET method implements new feature refinement modules, namely Dual Coordinate Attention Feature
Extraction (DCAFE) and Involution-Based Feature Refinement (Inv-FR). The DCAFE module utilizes the parallel
coordinate attention method using average and maximum pooling methods. The Inv-FR module further
enhances the spatial information by serial involution layers utilizing adaptive kernels. The Flora-NET method
has been experimentally trained and tested on two publicly available medicinal flower datasets. Extensive
experiments show that the Flora-NET method outperforms the existing deep neural classification methods by
a marginal accuracy of almost 6.50% and 5.59% on the two benchmark datasets. The effectiveness of the
Flora-NET method has also been validated by hyperparameter tuning, 𝑘-fold cross-validation, and ablation
studies. The code and datasets are available at https://github.com/ersachingupta11/Flora-NET.

1. Introduction
One of the foremost plant species, the flowers, is vital in maintaining
the ecological balance. Flowers may be utilized as the nourishment
powerhouse for human beings and are beneficial across diverse areas
for human use, such as food, clothing, medicines, and fertilizers (Lu
et al., 2022). For example, the flowers can improve water retention and
soil structure attributes by adding organic matter during decomposition, enabling it to be a natural and effective plant fertilization method.
Similarly, flowers have played an important role in the healthcare
sector due to the presence of antioxidants, antiviral, and antimicrobial
compounds. Medicinal flowers are a valuable source of Essential Oils
(EOs) with diverse bioactive compounds, offering remarkable efficacy
and benefits for the pharmaceutical sector. For instance, Nerium oleander L and Osmanthus fragrans species are used for cancer and
respiratory treatments in traditional medicine practices (Sharma et al.,
2023). Table 1 summarizes the usage of different medicinal flowers for
disease ailments. As per the World Health Organization (WHO) reports,

almost 80% population in developing countries rely on conventional
medicine practices where herbs are utilized for pain relief disease treatment (Lu et al., 2022). In India, nearly 70% of modern drugs related
to cardiovascular, cancer, and depression disorders are derived from
natural plant resources with several synthetic analogues made from
herb-based prototype compounds. Also, Ayurvedic medicine utilizes
around 1,200 to 1,800 flower species, while biologists and therapists
use over 7,500 different medicinal herbs in various treatments (Shi
et al., 2021). On the flip side, about 25% of these medicinal flower
species are at risk of extinction due to overharvesting, population
explosion, and loss of natural habitats. Additionally, one cannot afford
incorrect predictions in the medical domain because incorrect information can cause serious concerns for human beings. Thus, automated
medicinal flower identification is critical to preserve biological diversity, helping phytologists and healthcare professionals in the accurate
identification of medicinal flower properties.

exhibit similarities in shape and color with grass, leaves, and background, thus making it hard to identify (Shang et al., 2023). Moreover,
environmental factors like occlusions, deformation, viewpoint changes,
and varying image scales also pose challenges, which are further compounded by the complex backgrounds and diverse lighting conditions.

Different deep learning techniques like Convolution Neural Networks (CNNs) and their variants, transformers, and attention methods have shown remarkable presence in image classification, object
detection, and segmentation tasks. The high classification accuracy
of the ensemble methods has motivated the researchers to fuse features from different CNN models to identify flower species. Toğaçar
et al. (2020) used different pre-trained CNN architectures like AlexNet,
GoogLeNet, ResNet-50, and VGGNet-16 for efficient feature extraction,
which was further fed to Support Vector Machine (SVM) to classify
flower species. On the same footprints, Ghazi et al. (2017) combined
the feature space from three State-of-The-Art (SOTA) models, namely,
GoogLeNet, VGGNet-16, and AlexNet CNNs using transfer learning
and softmax classifier for flower identification. A lightweight customtailored deep neural model, ‘‘Ayur-PlantNet’’, was developed to classify
40 Ayurvedic plant herbs (Pushpa and Rani, 2023). The proposed
methods outperformed the different SOTA deep CNN models like VGGNet, ResNet, MobileNet, EfficientNet, and DenseNet. However, the
pre-trained models fail to capture the domain-specific feature vectors of flower species, thus showing poor generalization results. The
research enhanced the diverse feature extraction of the pre-trained
CNN models using a multiscale dilated convolution-based ensemble
learning method (You et al., 2022). The study showed the flower
classification accuracy improvement of about 4%–5%. However, the
dilated convolution might introduce gridding artifacts, causing loss of
finer details of flower species (Wang and Ji, 2018). Likewise, a multimodel CNN method was developed for flower classification using image
and text as input (Bae et al., 2020). The visual and textual features were
extracted using pre-trained CNNs, which were further concatenated
to another convolutional layer for better-integrated feature space. The
attention mechanism also plays a crucial role in determining the feature
importance along different spatial and channels for image classification

tasks. Zhang et al. (2021) introduced the spatial and channel attention
module in the pre-trained Xception architecture. The proposed method
obtained the diverse feature embedding space by joint optimization of
the softmax and triplet loss for flower classification. However, they
fail to capture the long-range dependencies in the feature space for
complex flower environments. A lightweight pre-trained ShuffleNet
network was employed utilizing the transfer learning methods for
flower classification (Fei et al., 2023). The proposed method implemented the ECANet attention mechanism to improve classification
accuracy. Zhao et al. (2020) introduced the novel CNN method by
incorporating the Part-information Perception Module (PPM) and the
Species Classification Module (SCM). It developed a novel Past Priority Attention Mechanism (PPAM) for effectively identifying the plant
herbs. However, the proposed method might overlook the global contextual information about the flower species that will contribute to
effectively identifying the medicinal flowers. The developed prototype
utilized the CSPDarnet53 & SSP modules for lightweight end-to-end
flower detection (Cheng and Zhang, 2020). The method exploited an
improved Spatial Attention Module (SAM) to determine the important
feature space for better detection accuracy. The research suggested
an improved framework concatenating the features from the residual
network and Convolutional Block Attention Module (CBAM) (Zhang
et al., 2023). Moreover, it integrated the Local Binary Pattern (LBP)
method to enhance the image understanding of the herb classification. However, the CBAM sequentially applies the channel and spatial
attention, thus limiting their interaction. (Yang and Ding, 2023) proposed a novel Multi-Graph Residual Attention Network and Semantic
Knowledge Fusion (SMRGAT) recommendation system that integrated
the semantic and external information about the herbs. It used a
multi-head attention mechanism to emphasize the most discriminative
feature space responsible for plant herd identification. The multi-head
attention method might store redundant or overlapped information as
each head computes the attention score independently.
Despite significant advances in cutting-edge smart technologies,
classifying flowers with diverse backgrounds remains a challenging
task. It is made obvious that the convolution operator has served as the
core element in deep learning-based models for image feature extraction (Yu and Cui, 2023). The spatial-agnostic and channel-dependent
properties of the convolution operation make it a suitable candidate
for image classification tasks. The convolution operation archives the
translation equivalence and improves the feature space representation by leveraging the shared kernel weights across each channel.
Moreover, the convolution collects diverse representation information
across different channels due to channel-specific property (Li et al.,
2021). Formally, the receptive field is directly proportional to the
kernel size for convolutional layers, thus allowing the larger kernels
to capture richer spatial context information in a single step. However,

expanding the receptive field by enlarging the kernel size significantly
increases the number of trainable and non-trainable parameters and
computational resources, which can complicate the training process.
Thus, the convolutions have a limited receptive field with no more
than 3 × 3 to maintain the trade-off between efficacy and computation. Convolution also neglects the long-range relevance representation
between different channels owing to the spatial-agnostic nature. Thus,
the convolution operation fails to capture global dependencies as it
inherently focuses on local neighbors (Li et al., 2021). Additionally,
the Attention Mechanism (AM) has shown a remarkable presence due
to the dynamic focus on the relevant region of interest from the whole
image. Existing attention mechanisms like Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE)
attention focus solely on encoding inter-channel information while ignoring the significance of the positional information. Also, they capture
the global information about the image instead of preserving the longrange detailed spatial relationships (Hou et al., 2021). These methods
fail to identify the medicinal flower with more spatial dependencies and
complex backgrounds to alleviate real-world problems in the medical
domain. Additionally, such morphological traits are easily influenced
by the image quality and often necessitate high-resolution images from
advanced camera devices (Yin et al., 2024). The global self-attention
method determines the pairwise attention scores between pixels of the
flower images, leading to quadratic computation cost. Thus, a novel DL
method must be deployed to integrate the global and local information
of the image for medicinal flower classification tasks. Involution and
coordinate attention methods may be a viable solution to maintain
the trade-off between the local and global feature space with minimal
computation cost.
The primary objective of the research work is to generate a coordinate attention-based involution kernel, which could dynamically
adapt the weights across different spatial positions to prioritize the
spatial context information. Also, the generated involution kernels
have been shared along the channel axis to manage the model parameters and computational complexity. Thus, the proposed method
has inverted the inherent convolution characteristics by utilizing the
channel-agnostic and spatial-dependent properties of the involution
operators. Additionally, the positional information loss has been alleviated by factorizing the channel attention map along horizontal and
vertical directions using two parallel 1D feature encoding processes.
The coordinate attention maps capture long-range dependencies in
each spatial direction, preserving positional information. The overall

method without increasing the model overhead (Hou et al., 2021).
The Flora-NET method utilizes the DCAFE module that incorporates
positional feature space into the channel attention mechanism, enhancing long-range dependencies without additional computational costs.

2.2.3. Involution-based feature refinement (Inv-FR) module
Involution kernels adopt inverse inherent properties of the convolution kernels, i.e., spatial-specific and channel-agnostic. It adaptively
generates dynamic kernel weights over different spatial positions and
shares the generated involution kernels across different channels (Li
et al., 2021). The Flora-NET method utilizes the Inv-FR module that
leverages the two involution layers to enable hierarchical feature extraction at different scales, which lets the model learn representations
that get more and more complicated and abstract. The Inv-FR module
preserves the hierarchical relationship crucial for medicinal flower
classification under a complex real-time environment. Considering the

4.2. Potential applications and impacts on the industry

An autonomous medicinal flower identification system across multiple classes remains a challenging task owing to similar feature vectors
among classes and the extensive intra-class variations. Conventional
deep learning methods have shown a decent performance in classification due to automatically extracting the deep feature vectors from the
given image, unlike traditional machine learning methods. However,
the flower classification task becomes more tedious in the case of
a real-time environment due to the occlusion of leaves, herbs, and
other vegetation. Additionally, multiple factors, such as deformation,
diverse lighting scenarios, and varying image scales, make the classification system more difficult. The deep neural methods fail to obtain
the spatially adaptive feature map extraction and accurate positional
context embedding, which can be overshadowed by the advanced
deep involution and coordinate attention methods. The research article
presents a novel Flora-NET method for medicinal flower classification,
which utilizes DCAFE and Inv-FR modules to determine better discrimination region and feature refinement for optimal classification
accuracy. The proposed DCAFE module has implemented the dual
coordinate attention method based on the average and max pooling
operations. Meanwhile, the proposed Inv-FR module incorporates a
series of involution layers for adaptively adjusting kernel filters to
capture the intricate flora features. The robust performance of the
Flora-NET method has been evaluated on two public flower datasets,
namely, Urban Street and Medicinal Blossom. Experimental results
show that the Flora-NET method has achieved the highest 91.12% and
91.18% classification accuracy for respective datasets, thus surpassing
the existing classification methods.

5. Conclusion and future perspectives

The proposed research on medicinal flower classification is crucial
for medicine manufacturing in the healthcare domain. Precise identification of medicinal flower species can simplify the selection process of
choosing the right medicinal plants for pharmaceutical production and
guarantee that appropriate ingredients are used to make medications.
It also helps in quality control, where high-quality flower species with
the desired medicinal properties have been selected for medicare purposes. Integrating the Flora-NET method in the pharmaceutical sector
can optimize the supply chain process by reducing the manual labor
of sorting and verifying medicinal species for large-scale production.
Misclassification of medicinal flowers can lead to harmful formulations
on a serious note, which can be minimized by integrating the Flora-NET
method. It can also be helpful in the cosmetic industry, ensuring the
correct usage of medicinal flower species for skincare products. The formulators can combine medicinal herbs containing anti-inflammatory,
antioxidant, and moisturizing properties to develop effective skincare
solutions. Medicinal flower species can be used to develop adequate
nutraceutical products in the food and beverage industry. They offer
functional, healthy foods along with basic nutritional values in the daily
consumption of foods. For instance, Flowering cherry can be used in
herbal teas to provide healthier flavor instead of artificial additives.
