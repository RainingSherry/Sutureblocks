Abstract
Vision Transformers (ViTs) mark a revolutionary advance
in neural networks with their token mixer’s powerful global
context capability. However, the pairwise token affinity and
complex matrix operations limit its deployment on resourceconstrained scenarios and real-time applications, such as mobile devices, although considerable efforts have been made
in previous works. In this paper, we introduce CAS-ViT:
Convolutional Additive Self-attention Vision Transformers,
to achieve a balance between efficiency and performance in
mobile applications. Firstly, we argue that the capability of token mixers to obtain global contextual information hinges on
multiple information interactions, such as spatial and channel
domains. Subsequently, we construct a novel additive similarity function following this paradigm and present an efficient implementation named Convolutional Additive Token
Mixer (CATM). This simplification leads to a significant reduction in computational overhead. We evaluate CAS-ViT
across a variety of vision tasks, including image classification, object detection, instance segmentation, and semantic
segmentation. Our experiments, conducted on GPUs, ONNX,
and iPhones, demonstrate that CAS-ViT achieves a competitive performance when compared to other state-of-the-art
backbones, establishing it as a viable option for efficient mobile vision applications. Our code and model are available at:
https://github.com/Tianfang-Zhang/CAS-ViT

Introduction
In recent years, the emergence of Vision Transformers
(ViTs) marks a revolutionary shift in neural network architecture (Han et al. 2021; Huang et al. 2023; Zhang
et al. 2023). Compared to Convolutional Neural Networks
(CNNs), which make a splash with lower computational
complexity and higher inference efficiency (Zhang et al.
2022; Zhang, Li, and Peng 2023), ViTs employ a novel
architecture known as token mixer (Yu et al. 2022). This
module facilitates enhanced global modeling and representational capabilities of ViTs through its ability to capture
long-range dependencies.
The basic module of ViT consists of token mixer, MLP,
and corresponding skip connections, where token mixer is
widely implemented as Multi-head Self-Attention (MSA).
MSA operates on the entire input sequence, compensating
for the limitations of CNNs with restricted receptive fields,

and providing unique advantages in terms of model scalability and adaptability (Li et al. 2023; Wu et al. 2024). Despite
its reputation, the reality that MSA is of quadratic complexity with respect to input image size (Wu et al. 2021) makes
ViT models more resource-intensive, rending it unsuitable
for real-time applications and unfavorable for widespread
deployment on resource-constrained devices, e.g. mobile
applications (Mehta and Rastegari 2021; Liu et al. 2023).
Therefore, developing token mixers that combine both efficient deployment and high performance becomes an urgent
problem on mobile devices.
Efforts have been made to improve token mixers, including MSA refinements and Heterogeneous-MSA (H-MSA).
Refinements focus on following matrix multiplication of
Query and Key to enhance the ability to capture long-range
dependencies while reducing algorithmic complexity. Specific techniques include feature shift (Liu et al. 2021), carrier token (Hatamizadeh et al. 2023), sparse attention (Wang
et al. 2022), linear attention (Han et al. 2023), etc. As an
alternative paradigm, H-MSA intends to break through the

limitations of point-by-point similarity between Q and K to
explore more flexible network designs (Yu et al. 2022). The
recently proposed pool token mixer (Yu et al. 2022) and context vector (Sandler et al. 2018; Shaker et al. 2023) further
accelerate the inference efficiency.
Although considerable progress has been made in related works, ViT models still suffer from the following constraints: 1. High complexity associated with matrix operations such as Softmax in token mixers. 2. The difficulty of
simultaneously achieving accuracy, efficiency and easy-todeployment on mobile devices or real-time applications.
In order to address the encountered problems, we propose
a family of lightweight networks named as Convolutional
Additive Self-attention (CAS)-ViT to achieve a balance between computation and efficiency as shown in Fig.1. Firstly,
we argue that the capability of token mixers to obtain global
contextual information hinges on multiple information interactions, such as spatial and channel domains. Meanwhile,
we construct a novel additive similarity function following
this paradigm, and expect to stimulate more valuable research. Further, we propose Convolutional Additive Token
Mixer (CATM) employing underlying spatial and channel
attention as novel interaction forms. This module eliminates
troublesome complex operations such as matrix multiplication and Softmax. Finally, we evaluate our method on various vision tasks, and the throughput is reported on GPUs,
ONNX and iPhone. Extensive experiments demonstrate our
competitive performance compared with other SOTA backbones.

Related Work
Efficient Vision Transformers
Since the debut of ViT (Dosovitskiy et al. 2020) and through
its successful validation in image classification tasks on
large-scale datasets such as ImageNet (Deng et al. 2009), it
has demonstrated the potential of self-attention mechanisms
for applications in computer vision (Dong et al. 2022). However, the concomitant increase in network sizes poses a huge
challenge for resource-constrained scenarios such as mobile
devices and real-time applications. To promote the potential
value of ViT, workers have invested a considerable effort in
efficient ViTs.
The improvement ideas for ViT architecture cover several aspects. One of which is to refine the token mixer
to enhance performance or solve the quadratic complexity problem of the self-attention mechanism. For example,
PVT (Wang et al. 2021) adopts spatial reduction strategy to
achieve sparse attention to deal with high-resolution images,
and Swin (Liu et al. 2021) employs a window-splitting approach to achieve local self-attention and deals with dependencies between patches by window shifting.
Another perspective is to explore hybrid models combining CNN and Transformer as compensation for the limitation of self-attention mechanism in processing local information (Guo et al. 2022). EdgeViT (Pan et al. 2022)
adopts convolutional layers and sparse attention for information integration and propagation in block respectively.
NextViT (Li et al. 2022a) confirms the validity of hybrid

models and the design strategy through comprehensive experiments. EfficientViT (Liu et al. 2023) further analyzes the
time-consuming proportion of various operations to achieve
efficient inference.

Efficient Token Mixer
Efficient token mixer design is one of the critical orientations
in the evolution of Transformers. Efforts have been made
to pursue lighter and more computationally efficient token
mixers to advance the feasibility of training and practical
applications.
One part of the effort is devoted to improving the MSA.
Twins (Chu et al. 2021) introduce self-attention between
patches to achieve global and local dependency tandem.
Works (Ali et al. 2021; Ding et al. 2022; Maaz et al.
2022) focus on information integration between channels
and achieve self-attention between distinct domains through
concatenation and transposition. Linear attention (Shen
et al. 2021) addresses the quadratic complexity of the selfattention mechanism by assuming that the similarity function is linearly differentiable. (Han et al. 2023) promotes
ReLU-based linear attention to be more discretized by focused function. (Bolya et al. 2022) further simplifies the attention complexity by increasing the attention heads.
Heteromorphic-MSA (H-MSA) is an extension form of
the self-attention mechanism development, which breaks
through the limitations of MSA framework and intended to
obtain better feature relations and more robust inference efficiency. Initially, MetaFormer (Yu et al. 2022) posited that
token mixer is not the key component that affects the performance of Transformers, however PoolFormer could not
be validated to be highly efficient. Subsequently, MobileViTv2 (Mehta and Rastegari 2022) simplifies the complex
matrix multiplication by endowing global information to
the context vector. SwiftFormer (Shaker et al. 2023) even
eliminates ’Value’ and implements weighted sums between
features with simpler normalization operations, achieving a
more concise and efficient H-MSA.

Convolutional Additive Self-attention
In this section, we argue that the information integration capability of self-attentive mechanisms resides in the multiple information interactions, e.g. the spatial association of
MSA, DaViT (Ding et al. 2022) in channels, and the compressive representation of MobileViTv2 and SwiftFormer in

both dimensions as in Fig. 2. Alternatively, isn’t it possible
that simple and efficient operations can work better while
satisfying multiple interactions?
Inheriting from this postulate, as shown in Fig. 2(d), we
innovatively define the similarity function as the sum of context scores of Q ∈ RN ×d and K ∈ RN ×d :
Sim (Q, K) = Φ(Q) + Φ(K) s.t. Φ(Q) = C(S(Q)) , (4)
where Query, Key, and Value are obtained by independent linear transformations, e.g. Q = Wq x, K = Wk x,
V = Wv x, Φ(·) denotes context mapping function, which
contains the essential information interactions. The advantage of this generalization is that is not restricted to manual context design, and confers the possibility of implementing by convolutional operations. In this article, we simply concretize Φ(·) as the Sigmoid-based channel attention
C(·) ∈ RN ×d and spatial attention S(·) ∈ RN ×d . Thus the
output of CATM can be formulated as:
O = Γ (Φ(Q) + Φ(K)) · V ,

where Γ(·) ∈ RN ×d denotes the linear transformation for
integrating the contextual information. Since the operations
in CATM are represented by convolution, the complexity is
O(N ).

Implementation Details ImageNet-1K (Deng et al. 2009)
contains over 1.3 million images across 1,000 natural categories. The dataset covers a wide range of objects and scenes
and has become one of the most extensively used datasets
with its diversity. We trained the network from scratch without any pre-trained models or additional data. The training
strategy follows EdgeNeXt (Maaz et al. 2022), all models are trained on input size of 224×224 using AdamW
(Loshchilov and Hutter 2018) optimizer for 300 epochs with
batch size of 2048. Learning rate is set to 6×10−3 and the
cosine (Loshchilov and Hutter 2016) decay schedule with
20 epochs warmup is used. Label smoothing 0.1 (Szegedy
et al. 2016), random resize crop, horizontal flip, RandAugment (Cubuk et al. 2020), multi-scale sampler (Mehta and
Rastegari 2021) are enabled, and the momentum of EMA
(Polyak and Juditsky 1992) is set to 0.9995 during training. To take full advantage of network effectiveness, we finetuned the model for another 30 epochs at a learning rate of
10−5 at 384×384 resolution with batch size of 64.
We implement the classification model on TIMM 1 based
on PyTorch (Paszke et al. 2019) and run on 16 V100 GPUs.
In addition, we compile the Torch model into ONNX format
and measure the throughput on V100 GPUs and Intel Xeon
Gold CPU @ 3.00GHz with batch size 64, respectively. For
the mobile side, we compiled it through CoreML library 2
and throughput is measured on deployment to iPhone X neural engine.

Limitation and Future Work
CAS-ViT makes extensive usage of convolution layers for
feature processing, which itself retains inductive bias. The
benefits include the ability to converge faster than vanilla
Transformer, and the consequent problem of being slightly
less effective on large-scale datasets and large parametric models. In future work, we will explore on larger
datasets and model scales. We are committed to developing
lightweight networks that are more efficient and convenient
to deploy.

In this paper, we proposed a convolutional additive selfattention network referred to as CAS-ViT. Firstly we argue that the key to enabling token mixer in ViT to work
efficiently is multiply information interaction including
spatial and channel domains. Subsequently following this
paradigm, we innovatively designed an additive similarity
function and implement it simply by Sigmoid-activated attention, which effectively avoids complex operations such as
matrix multiplication and Softmax in vanilla ViTs. We constructed a family of lightweight models and verified their
superior performance on tasks: image classification, object
detection, instance/semantic segmentation. Meanwhile, we
have deployed the network to ONNX and Iphone, and the
experiments demonstrate that CAS-ViT maintains high accuracy while facilitating efficient deployment and inference
on mobile devices.
